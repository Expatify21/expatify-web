We are ExPatify

Your new best friends in the Netherlands!

EXPATIFY is a brand that aims to help and empower expats as they navigate life in a foreign country. Our mission is to make expats feel connected and at home wherever they are, and our name represents the concept of a life without limits. We hope that our name conveys our concern for foreigners arriving in the Netherlands, as well as our desire to make them feel welcome and supported. Expatify recognizes the unique challenges and opportunities that come with living and working in a new country. As a result, we offer a variety of services tailored specifically to our target market, such as document translation, legalization, and authentication. We also encourage social networking among other expats living abroad.

BSN Number

The Burgerservicenummer, also known as the Citizen Service Number, is a unique identification number assigned to residents of the Netherlands. It's used to identify people in government systems and services like healthcare, taxes, and social security. This 9-digit number is used for a wide range of purposes, including healthcare, social security, taxes, education, and employment.

The BSN number is nine digits long and is assigned to people at birth or upon their arrival in the Netherlands. It is used for administrative purposes and is required for many official transactions, such as opening a bank account, enrolling in healthcare, or applying for a job.

Life Insurance

All residents are required to have basic health insurance, which covers essential healthcare such as visits to the general practitioner, hospital care, and prescription medications. You can select from a variety of insurance companies that provide basic health insurance. The government standardizes the coverage provided by all insurance companies, so the basic coverage is the same across all insurers. However, insurance costs can vary, so it is critical to compare policies and prices to find the best one for your needs.

If you require medical attention, you can go to any healthcare provider in the Netherlands who is recognized by your insurance company. However, you may be required to pay a small amount toward the cost of your treatment, referred to as your "own risk" or "deductible."
